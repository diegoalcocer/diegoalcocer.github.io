Nanay

When the world stopped, connection became essential. We created Nanay: a web platform that matched people asking for help with volunteers who could safely deliver it.

We designed every flow for clarity under pressure: categorized requests, verifiable identities, and transparent follow-ups so regulators, donors, and communities could trust the outcome.

Mutual aid Geo-located Verified volunteers
See it in the news
Meaning

Why we called it Nanay

In Quichua, Nanay means pain. In other languages, it evokes a caring mother. We embraced both ideas: naming a platform that listens to each story and responds with dignity.

Operations

More than an app

Nanay ran as an ecosystem: volunteers, donors, logistics teams, campaigns, and on-call operators coordinating everything remotely.

Volunteers Logistics Campaigns Operations Engineering
  • Onboarding & verifying helpers before any request went live.
  • Routing donations by proximity, language, and urgency.
  • Storytelling campaigns (like our Christmas drive) to keep help flowing.

The impact

In just a few months, Nanay gathered over 1,000 users.

We helped more than 250 families receive food, medicine, and essentials, right when they needed them most.

Our final campaign became our proudest moment: a Christmas drive that brought hope to 90+ families through community donations.

Every delivery was personal. Every action, human.
