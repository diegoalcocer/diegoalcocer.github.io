Nanay

When the world stopped, connection became essential.

In the middle of the pandemic, I led the creation of Nanay, a web platform that connected people who needed help with volunteers ready to offer it.

The idea was simple.

Building it was hard.

A name with meaning

In Quichua, Nanay means pain.

In other languages, it means mother.

For us, it meant both: understanding people’s needs, and responding with care.

The impact

In just a few months, Nanay gathered over 1,000 users.

We helped more than 250 families receive food, medicine, and essentials, right when they needed them most.

Our final campaign became our proudest moment: a Christmas drive that brought hope to 90+ families through community donations.

Every delivery was personal. Every action, human.

Connected with 1,000+ registered users ready to help.

Brought essentials to 250+ families—food, medicine, and care.

Delivered holiday kits to 90+ families through community donations.

Behind the scenes

Nanay wasn’t just an app. It was an ecosystem. We managed volunteers, coordinated donations, handled logistics, built campaigns, and kept everything running in real time while working remotely and under strict restrictions.

Grateful for the incredible Nanay team who showed up every day with heart and grit.
